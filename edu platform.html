What is Cybercrimes?

Cybercrimes, also known as computer crimes or digital crimes, are illegal activities that involve the use of computers or the internet to commit offenses.

These crimes typically target computer systems, networks, or digital information. Cybercrimes can range from theft of personal or financial information to attacks on computer systems, and can have severe consequences for individuals, organizations, and governments.
Cybercrimes covers a wide range of illegal activities carried out using computers or the internet. Here are some common types:

Phishing

Definition: Phishing is a cybercrime in which a target is contacted by email, phone, or text by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data.

Examples: Fake emails from banks, deceptive websites that look real, and fake customer support calls.

Causes: Lack of awareness, curiosity, and clicking on unverified links.

Effects: Loss of personal data, financial loss, identity theft.

Preventive Measures: Verify email sources, do not click on unknown links, and report suspicious messages.

Hacking

Definition: Hacking involves unauthorized access to computer systems or networks to steal, alter, or destroy data.

Examples: Unauthorized access to emails, breaking into corporate networks, exploiting software vulnerabilities.

Causes: Lack of security awareness, software vulnerabilities, weak passwords, social engineering.

Effects: Loss of personal data, financial loss, identity theft.

Preventive Measures: Implement strong security protocols, use complex passwords, educate users, and monitor systems for suspicious activity.

Malware

Definition: Malware is malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems.

Examples: Viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware.

Causes: Downloading infected files, visiting malicious websites.

Effects: Data loss, system damage, financial loss.

Preventive Measures: Use antivirus software, avoid downloading from untrusted sources, regularly update software.

Social Engineering

Definition: Social engineering is the technique used by attackers to manipulate individuals to disclose confidential information.

Examples: Impersonating authority figures, exploiting human psychology.

Causes: Trusting unfamiliar individuals, lack of awareness.

Effects: Unauthorized access, data breaches.

Preventive Measures: Be cautious with sharing information, verify identities of requesters.

Ways and Measures To Prevent Cyber Attacks

There are numerous ways to prevent cyber attacks; among them are:

Password Management

Definition: Password management involves creating and storing strong, unique passwords for all accounts to prevent unauthorized access.

Examples: Using password managers, avoiding common passwords like '123456' or 'password' , enable two-factor authentication, and update passwords regularly.

Data Encryption

Definition: Data encryption is the process of converting data into a secure format to prevent unauthorized access.

Examples: AES(Advanced Encryption Standard) , RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman), use encrypted communication channels like,

HTTPS(Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure), PGP(Pretty Good Privacy), VPNs(Virtual Private Networks), Ecnrypted messaging apps(Whatsapp and Telegram), Encrypted voice and video calls apps(zoom and microsoft teams).
